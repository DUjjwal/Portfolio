Week 1 :


We had a discussion on the topic "Practical for Employability Skills," where our teacher asked us to share our views on how this subject would help us. We also discussed the kind of professionals we aspire to be after completing the course, along with the skills we hope to develop or improve. Our teacher shared some motivational stories to inspire us. Additionally, we took part in an activity where we wrote about where we envision ourselves in two years—focusing on our knowledge, skills, and attitude.

Week 2 :


In today’s session, we were divided into groups of 6 or 7 members and seated in circles. We discussed SWOC analysis—a framework for evaluating Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Challenges. After understanding the concept, each group member shared their personal SWOC analysis with the team. We also named our group—ours was "Essentialist" Later, as part of an activity, we were assigned to record a group video discussing a selected topic collaboratively.

Week 3 :


During this session, we reconvened in our previous groups, sitting in a circular formation. The focus of today's discussion was the Johari Window—a psychological model aimed at enhancing self-awareness and interpersonal understanding. We learned that it categorizes self-perception into four key areas: the open self (known to self and others), the blind self (unknown to self but seen by others), the hidden self (known only to oneself), and the unknown self (traits undiscovered by both self and others).

Week 4 :


During today's class, we delved deeper into "Presentation Skills," exploring the VAK learning styles—Visual, Auditory, and Kinesthetic (hands-on learning). These styles help us understand different ways people absorb and retain information. We also examined essential components of impactful presentations, including: Clarity and conciseness in content Storytelling techniques to engage listeners Delivery methods to maintain audience interest As a practical exercise, we wrote a one-page story on "The Role of Technology in Business" to apply these concepts.
